The Rise of Obesity in New York State

By: Jenna Bagcal

The New York State Department of Health compiled data which shows the rise of obesity from 2000 to 2010.

According to the data, the rate of obsesity has risen from 17.7 percent to 24.5 percent, which is a 40 percent increase.

The bar chart below is a visual representation of obesity's upward trend.

Another key finding in the NYSDOA data is that the highest rate of obesity in the five boroughs is in the Bronx, while the lowest is in Manhattan. The Bronx has 31 percent obesity among its population, which is above the New York State percentage of 24.5 percent. In Manhattan, the percentage of obese adults is below the state average, at 16.8 percent.

The data also showed that non-Hispanic Blacks have 32.5 percent in New York. Other groups who have high obesity rates are those who have less than $25,000 annual income and individuals with disabilities.
